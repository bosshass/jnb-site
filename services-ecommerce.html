Amazon Seller Accounting: Why It's Different

Traditional bookkeeping doesn't work for Amazon sellers. The complexity of FBA fees, multi-state sales tax, inventory accounting, and constant fee changes requires specialized ecommerce accounting expertise.

The Amazon Fee Problem

Amazon charges referral fees, FBA fulfillment fees, storage fees, long-term storage fees, removal fees, disposal fees, advertising fees, and more. These fees vary by category, product size, and time of year. Without proper Amazon fee tracking and analysis, you can't calculate true product profitability.

Inventory Accounting for Ecommerce

Ecommerce inventory accounting requires tracking cost of goods sold (COGS) across multiple warehouses, understanding landed costs including freight and duties, and reconciling physical inventory with Amazon's records. We help Amazon sellers implement proper inventory valuation methods and track COGS accurately.

Amazon Data Analysis for Wholesalers

Wholesale Amazon sellers face unique challenges: thin margins, high volume, and complex supplier relationships. Our Amazon data mining capabilities help wholesalers identify which products and suppliers are truly profitable after all Amazon fees are accounted for. We analyze your Amazon Business Reports, Settlement Reports, and Inventory Reports to build a complete picture of wholesale profitability.

Multi-Channel Ecommerce Accounting

Selling on Amazon, Shopify, Walmart Marketplace, and eBay? We consolidate financial data across all channels, track inventory across warehouses, and provide unified reporting so you can see your entire ecommerce business in one place.
