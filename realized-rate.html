Realized Rate

What you actually collect per hour worked

Your Rate Sheet Is Fiction

You say you charge $85/hour. But what do you actually collect per hour worked?

The realized rate is the truth. It accounts for discounts, write-offs, scope creep, unbilled time, and everything else that erodes your posted rate.

The Formula

Realized Rate = Total Revenue ÷ Total Hours Worked

Not billable hours. Not the hours on the invoice. The actual hours your people spent on that work.

What Kills Your Realized Rate

  • Discounts: "We'll take 10% off to get the deal"
  • Scope creep: Extra work you didn't bill for
  • Write-offs: Hours you worked but wrote off to keep the client happy
  • Fixed-price overruns: Jobs that took longer than quoted
  • Unbilled travel: Drive time you absorbed
  • Callbacks: Coming back to fix something at no charge

Most owners think they're billing $80-100/hour. When they calculate realized rate, it's often $55-70. That gap is where profit disappears.

Compare to Floor Rate

Here's where it gets real:

  • Realized rate > Floor rate: You're making money
  • Realized rate = Floor rate: You're breaking even
  • Realized rate < Floor rate: You're losing money on every hour

Bottom Line

  • Calculate realized rate monthly: Revenue ÷ Hours Worked
  • Compare it to your floor rate
  • The gap tells you how much margin you're actually making (or losing)
  • Track by service type, by client, by team member
