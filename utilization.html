Utilization

The gap between paid time and billed time

The Hidden Leak

You pay people for 40 hours a week. How many of those hours actually show up on an invoice?

The gap between paid hours and billed hours is utilization. And it's where most service businesses leak profit without realizing it.

The Formula

Utilization = Billable Hours ÷ Paid Hours × 100

If someone works 40 hours but only 28 are billable, that's 70% utilization. You're paying for 12 hours of... what exactly?

Where Non-Billable Time Goes

  • Travel: Driving between jobs (if not billed)
  • Admin: Paperwork, timesheets, emails
  • Meetings: Team meetings, client calls that aren't billed
  • Rework: Fixing mistakes, callbacks
  • Bench time: Waiting for the next job
  • Training: Learning new skills

Reality check: Most service businesses run 60-75% utilization. Below 60% means you're paying people to wait. Above 80% usually means burnout or fudged timesheets.

Why 5% Matters

A 5% improvement in utilization—from 65% to 70%—can mean thousands in recovered revenue. You're not working more hours, you're just billing more of the hours you're already paying for.

Bottom Line

  • Track billable vs. paid hours weekly, per person
  • Target 70-75% for most service businesses
  • Below 60% = major profit leak
  • Every 5% improvement goes straight to the bottom line
