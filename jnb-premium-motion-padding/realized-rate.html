Realized Rate

Your target rate vs. what you actually collect. The report card that tells you the truth.

The Rate You Quote Isn't the Rate You Get

You bill $100/hour. At least, that's what you tell clients. But what do you actually collect when you divide revenue by hours worked?

For most service businesses, the realized rate is 15-30% lower than the target rate. That's not margin — that's disappearing profit.

Where Rates Erode

Scope Creep

"While you're here, can you just..." Those five words cost you more money than any single expense on your P&L. A 2-hour job becomes 3 hours. You bill for 2. Your realized rate just dropped 33%.

Fixed-Fee Traps

You quote $500 for a job. It takes 6 hours instead of 4. You just worked for $83/hour instead of $125/hour. Fixed fees only work if you're extremely accurate at estimating — and most businesses aren't.

Discounts

"10% off for returning customers." That sounds like good marketing. But if your margin is 20%, a 10% discount cuts your profit in half.

Unbilled Time

The 15-minute call to clarify something. The drive back to grab a forgotten tool. The time spent writing up the invoice. It all adds up, and none of it gets billed.

The Calculation

Realized Rate = Total Revenue Collected ÷ Total Hours Worked (billable + unbilled)

Example

Target rate: $100/hour

  • Billed: 120 hours @ $100 = $12,000
  • Scope creep (unbilled): 15 hours = $0
  • Discounts given: $600
  • Total collected: $11,400
  • Total hours worked: 135

Realized rate: $84.44/hour

You're leaving $15.56 per hour on the table. On 135 hours, that's $2,100/month in lost revenue.

Bottom Line

  • Realized rate = what you actually collect per hour worked
  • Track it monthly — it's your pricing report card
  • Scope creep is the biggest rate killer
  • If realized rate is below floor rate, you're losing money on every job
