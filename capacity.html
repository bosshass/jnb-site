Capacity Planning

When to hire, when to hold, when you're underwater

The Hiring Trap

You're slammed. Everyone's working overtime. Clients are waiting. Obviously you need to hire, right?

Not so fast. Hiring based on "we're busy" is how service businesses blow themselves up.

Know Your Capacity First

Capacity = Team Members × Billable Hours per Person × Utilization Target

If you have 3 techs, each with 1,600 available billable hours at 70% utilization, your capacity is 3,360 billable hours per year.

The Three Zones

  • Under 70% of capacity: You have room. Don't hire—sell.
  • 70-85% of capacity: Healthy. Start thinking about your next hire.
  • Over 85% of capacity: Danger zone. Quality suffers, people burn out, clients get frustrated.

The mistake: hiring when you're at 90% capacity because you "need help now." By the time they're trained, you're back to 70% and can't afford them.

When to Actually Hire

  • You've been at 80%+ capacity for 3+ months
  • You have pipeline visibility showing sustained demand
  • You can afford the new person at 50% utilization for 6 months (training ramp)
  • Your floor rate math still works with the added cost

Bottom Line

  • Know your capacity number—don't guess
  • Being "busy" isn't a reason to hire
  • Hire when you're sustainably at 80%+ with pipeline to back it
  • Budget 6 months of ramp time in your math
