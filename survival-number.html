The Survival Number

What your business needs monthly just to stay alive

Beyond Break-Even

Floor rate tells you what each billable hour costs. But your business has expenses that exist whether anyone's billing or not.

The survival number is what you need to cover every month before you think about profit, growth, or paying yourself well.

What Goes Into the Survival Number

  • Fixed overhead: Rent, insurance, utilities, subscriptions—the stuff that bills you whether you work or not
  • Debt service: Loan payments, equipment financing, lines of credit
  • Minimum owner's draw: What you need to take home to keep your life running
  • Taxes: Set aside 25-30% of profit or you'll regret it in April
  • Emergency buffer: 10% cushion for the stuff you forgot

Most owners dramatically underestimate their survival number because they forget debt service, taxes, and their own minimum needs.

The Formula

Survival Number = Fixed Costs + Debt + Owner's Draw + Tax Reserve + 10% Buffer

This is your monthly nut. The number you need to hit before anything else matters.

Why This Matters

When you know your survival number, you can answer critical questions:

  • How many billable hours do we need this month?
  • Can we afford to lose this client?
  • What happens if we have a slow month?
  • How much runway do we have in the bank?

Bottom Line

  • Survival number = everything you must cover monthly, no exceptions
  • Include owner's draw—you need to eat too
  • Add a 10% buffer for reality
  • This is the floor for your business, not your goals
