Utilization

The silent killer. Why being "busy" doesn't mean being profitable.

The Busy Trap

Your team is slammed. Everyone's working full days. You should be making money, right?

Not necessarily.

Utilization is the percentage of available hours that are actually billable. And for most service businesses, it's shockingly low.

The Math That Breaks Your Business

You're billing $100/hour. You have a tech working 40 hours a week. That should be $4,000/week, $16,000/month in revenue.

But here's reality:

  • Drive time: 6 hours/week (not billable)
  • Callbacks/warranty: 3 hours/week (not billable)
  • Admin/paperwork: 4 hours/week (not billable)
  • Waiting on parts/customers: 2 hours/week (not billable)
  • Training/meetings: 2 hours/week (not billable)

That's 17 hours of non-billable time. Your tech is only billing 23 hours out of 40.

Utilization: 57.5%

Your $16,000/month revenue target? It's actually $9,200. Your profit margin just got cut in half and you didn't even notice.

Why Firing People Doesn't Fix It

When revenue is tight, the instinct is to cut staff. But if the problem is utilization, cutting people doesn't help — you still have the same percentage of non-billable time.

If your 3-person team has 55% utilization, firing one person gives you a 2-person team with 55% utilization. You haven't solved anything.

Where Utilization Hides

  • Poor scheduling: Gaps between jobs, inefficient routing
  • Scope creep: Work that gets done but not billed
  • Callbacks: Fixing mistakes on your dime
  • Admin burden: Paperwork that should be automated
  • Training: Necessary but not billable

Bottom Line

  • Utilization = Billable Hours ÷ Available Hours
  • Most service businesses run 50-65% utilization
  • Every 10% improvement goes straight to margin
  • Busy ≠ Billable — track both separately
